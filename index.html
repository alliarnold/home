Alli Arnold (she/her) is an arts worker and MS candidate in the Grad Center at CUNY’s Data Analysis & Visualization program. Her arts administration experience spans from managing public art projects to exhibition production, collection management, and public programming to producing fine print editions and auctions.

Alli holds a BA in Art History and French from Loyola University Chicago and an MA in the Connoisseurship of Fine & Decorative Art from the University of Manchester's Sotheby's Institute of Art program. She has worked in various programming and curatorial capacities at cultural institutions around New York City including A.I.R. Gallery, the Brooklyn Academy of Music, and the MTA’s Arts & Design team.

As a data enthusiast, Alli has overseen the design and build out of several custom art collection management databases, researched climate solutions, and created engaging visuals on topics ranging from audience engagement to aquaculture.

In her free time, Alli can be found reading a good cozy mystery, looking up tree facts, and searching for the perfect cookie recipe.
